Hypercholesterolemia (Hyperlipidemia)

Definition

Dyslipidemia refers to abnormal concentrations of lipids in the blood. Hyperlipidemia indicates elevated levels of cholesterol and/or triglycerides.

  • Hypercholesterolemia: Elevated total or LDL cholesterol
  • Hypertriglyceridemia: Elevated triglycerides
  • Mixed hyperlipidemia: Both cholesterol and triglycerides elevated

Etiology

CategoryExamples
Primary (genetic) Familial hypercholesterolemia, familial combined hyperlipidemia, polygenic hypercholesterolemia
Secondary (acquired) Uncontrolled diabetes mellitus, hypothyroidism, nephrotic syndrome, chronic kidney disease, Cushing syndrome, alcohol use, obesity, medications (e.g., corticosteroids, thiazides, antiretrovirals)

Screening

  • Adults: Begin at age 20; repeat every 4–6 years if normal (per ACC/AHA).
  • High-risk groups: Family history of premature ASCVD, diabetes, hypertension, obesity — screen earlier and more frequently.
  • Children: Universal screening once between ages 9–11 and 17–21 (for familial forms).

Clinical Features

  • Usually asymptomatic
  • Xanthomas: yellow papules over tendons or skin
  • Xanthelasma: cholesterol deposits on eyelids
  • Corneal arcus: white/gray ring around cornea (early sign in familial hypercholesterolemia)
  • Complications: atherosclerosis → coronary artery disease, stroke, peripheral vascular disease

Diagnosis

  • Lipid panel: fasting or non-fasting (total cholesterol, LDL, HDL, triglycerides)
  • LDL calculation: LDL = Total cholesterol − HDL − (Triglycerides/5)
  • Consider secondary causes with: fasting glucose, TSH, liver and renal function tests

Treatment

1. Lifestyle Modifications (for all patients)

  • Diet: reduce saturated fats, trans fats, and cholesterol intake
  • Increase fiber, fruits, vegetables, and omega-3 intake
  • Regular aerobic exercise (≥150 min/week)
  • Weight control and smoking cessation
  • Limit alcohol intake

2. Pharmacologic Therapy

Drug ClassExamplesMain Effects / Notes
Statins (HMG-CoA reductase inhibitors) Atorvastatin, Rosuvastatin, Simvastatin ↓ LDL 30–60%; first-line for ASCVD prevention; monitor LFTs and myopathy
Ezetimibe — ↓ LDL 15–25%; add if statin alone insufficient or statin intolerance
PCSK9 inhibitors Evolocumab, Alirocumab Potent LDL reduction (~60%); for familial or refractory hypercholesterolemia
Bile acid sequestrants Cholestyramine, Colesevelam ↓ LDL; may ↑ triglycerides; safe in pregnancy
Fibrates Fenofibrate, Gemfibrozil ↓ Triglycerides; avoid combining Gemfibrozil with statins
Niacin — ↑ HDL, ↓ LDL and triglycerides; limited by flushing, hyperglycemia
Omega-3 fatty acids Icosapent ethyl ↓ Triglycerides; used in severe hypertriglyceridemia

3. Treatment Targets

  • Severe LDL elevation (≥190 mg/dL): High-intensity statin
  • Familial hypercholesterolemia: LDL goal < 100 mg/dL (or < 70 mg/dL if ASCVD)
  • High triglycerides (>500 mg/dL): Fibrates or omega-3s to prevent pancreatitis

4. Follow-Up

  • Repeat lipid panel 6–12 weeks after initiation or dose change
  • Monitor liver enzymes and muscle symptoms
  • Reassess cardiovascular risk and adherence regularly

Familial Hypercholesterolemia (FH)

  • Autosomal dominant disorder → defective LDL receptor
  • LDL typically > 190 mg/dL in adults
  • Early-onset CAD, tendon xanthomas, corneal arcus
  • Treatment: maximal statin + ezetimibe ± PCSK9 inhibitor; consider LDL apheresis in homozygous FH

Severe Hypertriglyceridemia

  • Triglycerides > 500 mg/dL → risk of pancreatitis
  • Treat secondary causes (alcohol, diabetes, drugs)
  • Start fibrate ± omega-3 fatty acids
  • Add niacin or statin if LDL elevation coexists

Xanthomas and Xanthelasma

  • Cutaneous manifestations of lipid accumulation
  • May regress with lipid control, but often recur
  • Removal (laser/surgical) for cosmetic purposes

Cardiovascular Risk Calculator (ASCVD) & Statin Therapy Decision Guide

1. ASCVD 10-Year Risk Estimation

  • Use the ASCVD-Risk-calculator-2013(ACC/AHA).
  • Includes: age, sex, race, total & HDL cholesterol, systolic BP, diabetes, smoking status, and BP treatment.

2. Statin Therapy Recommendations (ACC/AHA 2018)

Risk Group / ConditionRecommendation
Clinical ASCVD High-intensity statin (goal ≥50% LDL reduction)
LDL ≥ 190 mg/dL High-intensity statin; consider PCSK9 inhibitor if not controlled
Diabetes (age 40–75) Moderate- to high-intensity statin regardless of calculated risk
No diabetes, age 40–75, LDL 70–189 mg/dL Base on 10-year ASCVD risk:
• <5%: lifestyle only
• 5–7.5%: consider moderate statin
• 7.5–20%: moderate to high statin
• >20%: high-intensity statin
Adults >75 years Individualized decision based on benefit vs. risk

3. Statin Intensity (Typical Dosing)

IntensityStatin Examples
High Atorvastatin 40–80 mg, Rosuvastatin 20–40 mg
Moderate Atorvastatin 10–20 mg, Rosuvastatin 5–10 mg, Simvastatin 20–40 mg, Pravastatin 40–80 mg
Low Simvastatin 10 mg, Pravastatin 10–20 mg, Lovastatin 20 mg

4. Monitoring & Follow-up

  • Recheck lipids 6–12 weeks after therapy initiation or adjustment.
  • Assess adherence and tolerance at every visit.
  • Recalculate ASCVD risk every 4–6 years in adults 20–79 years without ASCVD.
