Hypothyroidism vs Hyperthyroidism

Aspect Hypothyroidism Hyperthyroidism
Definition Condition of insufficient thyroid hormone production leading to slowed metabolism. Condition of excessive thyroid hormone production leading to increased metabolism.
Common Causes Autoimmune thyroiditis (Hashimoto’s), iodine deficiency, post-thyroidectomy, thyroiditis, drugs. Graves' disease (autoimmune stimulation), toxic multinodular goiter, toxic adenoma, thyroiditis, excess hormone intake.
Symptoms Fatigue, weight gain, cold intolerance, constipation, dry skin, bradycardia, depression, menstrual irregularities. Weight loss despite appetite increase, heat intolerance, palpitations, tremor, anxiety, diarrhea, sweating, goiter.
Physical Exam Coarse skin, facial puffiness, bradycardia, delayed reflexes, non-pitting edema (myxedema). Tachycardia, tremors, warm moist skin, exophthalmos (Graves'), goiter.
Lab Findings High TSH (primary), low free T4/T3, positive anti-thyroid peroxidase (TPO) antibodies in Hashimoto’s. Low TSH, high free T4/T3, positive TSH receptor antibodies (TRAb) in Graves’ disease.
Diagnosis Tools TSH, free T4, thyroid autoantibodies, ultrasound for goiter or nodules. TSH, free T4/T3, TRAb antibodies, radioactive iodine uptake scan (high in Graves, low in thyroiditis), ultrasound.
Treatment Levothyroxine replacement therapy; adjust dose based on TSH monitoring. Antithyroid drugs (methimazole, PTU), radioactive iodine ablation, beta-blockers for symptom control, thyroidectomy in select cases.
Complications Myxedema coma (severe hypothyroidism), cardiovascular disease, infertility, developmental delay (congenital cases). Thyroid storm (emergency), atrial fibrillation, osteoporosis, ophthalmopathy (in Graves’).
Prognosis Good with lifelong hormone replacement and monitoring. Good if treated early; untreated can be life-threatening.
