Adult Vaccination in Saudi Arabia

Overview

Adult vaccination is a critical part of preventive health care. The following schedule aligns with recommendations from the Saudi Ministry of Health (MOH), CDC Adult Immunization Schedule, and AMBOSS Preventive Medicine Guidelines.

Vaccines are tailored based on age, comorbidities, occupation, travel, and risk factors.

Core Vaccines Recommended for All Healthy Adults

Vaccine Indication / Schedule Notes
Influenza (Flu) Annually, before winter season (October–December) All adults, especially ≥50 years, health care workers, chronic illness
Tdap (Tetanus, Diphtheria, Pertussis) Single Tdap dose in adulthood, then Td/Tdap booster every 10 years Important for health care workers and those in contact with infants
COVID-19 Primary series + booster per MOH updates Strongly recommended for all adults and travelers
MMR (Measles, Mumps, Rubella) 2 doses, ≥4 weeks apart if not previously immunized Required for adults born after 1970 without documented immunity
Varicella (Chickenpox) 2 doses, ≥4 weeks apart if no prior infection or vaccination Test serology if uncertain immunity

Vaccines for Specific Risk Groups

Vaccine Indicated for Schedule
Hepatitis B All adults not previously vaccinated, especially health care workers, diabetics, or those with chronic liver disease 3 doses (0, 1, and 6 months)
Hepatitis A Travelers to endemic areas, chronic liver disease, food handlers 2 doses (0 and 6–12 months)
Pneumococcal Adults ≥50 years or with chronic diseases (heart, lung, diabetes, renal, asplenia) PCV20 single dose
or PCV15 followed by PPSV23 (after 1 year)
Meningococcal ACWY Hajj or Umrah pilgrims, health care workers, students in dormitories, travelers to endemic areas Single dose every 5 years for at-risk individuals
HPV (Human Papillomavirus) Females and males aged 9–45 years 2 or 3 doses depending on age (0, 1–2, 6 months)
Shingles (Herpes Zoster) Adults ≥50 years Recombinant zoster vaccine (2 doses, 2–6 months apart)

Special Populations

  • Pregnant women: Tdap (during each pregnancy, 27–36 weeks), influenza (any trimester).
  • Healthcare workers: Influenza, Tdap, Hepatitis B, MMR, Varicella, COVID-19.
  • Chronic diseases (e.g., diabetes, CKD, COPD): Influenza, pneumococcal, hepatitis B.
  • Travelers: Meningococcal ACWY (mandatory for Hajj/Umrah), Hepatitis A/B, Yellow fever (if indicated).

Documentation and Counseling

  • Record all vaccines in the MOH Sehaty app or patient’s immunization card.
  • Provide education on side effects (local pain, mild fever).
  • Encourage adherence to boosters and travel vaccine updates.
