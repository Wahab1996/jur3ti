Approach to a Patient with Decreased Vision

1. Initial Evaluation

History

  • Onset (sudden vs. gradual)
  • Unilateral vs. bilateral
  • Associated symptoms (pain, flashes, floaters, headache)
  • Past ocular or systemic diseases (diabetes, hypertension)
  • Medication and trauma history

Physical Examination

  • Visual acuity – Use Snellen, E, or Landolt charts at 6 m (20 ft). Test each eye separately and record as 20/x.
  • Pupils – Assess for size, symmetry, and light reaction.
  • Visual fields – Confrontational testing with a finger or pen.
  • Extraocular movements – Check for restriction or diplopia.
  • Inspection – Evaluate eyelids, sclera, and conjunctiva.
  • Fundoscopy – Look for papilledema, hemorrhages, or detachment if indicated.

2. Common Causes of Decreased Vision

Category Typical Pattern
Refractive errors (myopia, hyperopia, astigmatism) Gradual, bilateral, improved with pinhole
Cataract Gradual, glare, blurred vision
Glaucoma Peripheral field loss (tunnel vision)
Macular degeneration Central vision loss, often in elderly
Retinal detachment Sudden, curtain-like shadow, flashes/floaters
Vascular occlusion Sudden, painless complete loss (artery) or patchy (vein)
Optic neuritis Painful, acute vision loss, often in young adults

3. When to Refer Urgently

  • Sudden vision loss (monocular or binocular)
  • Painful eye with decreased vision
  • Red eye with visual impairment
  • Flashes or floaters with visual field defect
  • Abnormal red reflex (e.g., leukocoria)
  • Ocular trauma
  • Abnormal pupil responses (e.g., RAPD)
  • Papilledema or suspected retinal detachment

Routine Referral

  • Progressive refractive changes uncorrectable with pinhole
  • Chronic, unexplained visual decline
  • Suspected cataract or glaucoma

4. Visual Acuity as a Guide

Snellen Acuity Interpretation / Action
20/20–20/40 Normal or mild impairment – routine care
Worse than 20/40 Requires refraction or ophthalmology referral
20/200 or worse Severe visual impairment / legal blindness – refer urgently
Asymmetry > 2 lines between eyes Suggests pathology – refer
