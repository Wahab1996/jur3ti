Case 1: Short Clinical Note (SOAP Style)

Subjective

Pt c/o mild shortness of breath and dry cough for 2 days. No chest pain, no fever.

Objective (On Examination)

General: Pt looks well oriented, conscious, not in respiratory distress, no cyanosis, no pallor, no dehydration.

Throat: Clear, no tonsillar enlargement.

Chest: Air entry equal bilaterally, no wheeze, no crackles.

CVS: S1, S2 heard, no murmurs.

Abdomen: Soft, non-tender, no organomegaly.

Extremities: No edema, peripheral pulses palpable.

Assessment

Likely mild upper respiratory tract infection (URTI).

Plan

  • Symptomatic treatment (hydration, rest).
  • Monitor for fever or worsening cough.
  • Follow-up in 3 days or earlier if symptoms progress.

Case 2: Full Physical Examination

General Appearance

Pt is alert, oriented ×3, well-nourished, in no acute distress. No pallor, jaundice, cyanosis, clubbing, or pedal edema.

Vital Signs

  • Temp: 36.8 °C
  • Pulse: 78 bpm, regular

Conscious, oriented, GCS 15/15. Cranial nerves II–XII intact. Motor power 5/5 in all limbs. Reflexes normal and symmetrical. Sensation intact.

Extremities

Skin

Assessment

Normal physical examination findings.
