Hypertension (HTN)

Definition

Hypertension is defined as persistently elevated blood pressure. According to the 2017 ACC/AHA guidelines, hypertension in adults is diagnosed when blood pressure ≥ 130/80 mmHg. The JNC 8 guidelines define it as ≥ 140/90 mmHg.

Classification

  • Primary (Essential) Hypertension: ~90% of cases, no identifiable cause.
  • Secondary Hypertension: due to an underlying disease (renal, endocrine, vascular).

Common Causes of Secondary Hypertension

  • Chronic kidney disease
  • Primary hyperaldosteronism
  • Renal artery stenosis
  • Coarctation of the aorta
  • Endocrine disorders (e.g., Cushing’s, pheochromocytoma, thyroid disease)

Clinical Features

  • Often asymptomatic until organ damage occurs.
  • Possible early symptoms: headache, dizziness, tinnitus, chest discomfort.

Target organs affected: brain, heart, kidneys, eyes (e.g., retinopathy, stroke, MI).

Diagnosis

  • Persistent elevated BP on ≥2 separate occasions in the office.
  • Confirm with out-of-office BP measurement (home or ambulatory monitoring).
  • Assess cardiovascular risk and screen for end-organ damage.
  • Investigate secondary causes if suspected.
Test Purpose
Basic metabolic panel (electrolytes, creatinine) Evaluate renal function and electrolytes.
Urinalysis Detect proteinuria or hematuria.
Lipid panel Assess cardiovascular risk.
ECG Detect LV hypertrophy or arrhythmias.
Fundoscopic exam Look for hypertensive retinopathy.

Treatment

1. Lifestyle Modifications

  • Weight reduction and regular exercise.
  • DASH diet (low sodium, high fruits/vegetables).
  • Limit alcohol intake and quit smoking.

2. Pharmacologic Therapy

  • First-line agents: ACE inhibitors, ARBs, thiazide diuretics, calcium channel blockers.
  • Drug choice depends on comorbidities and patient characteristics.
  • Example starting doses (general adult):
MedicationTypical Starting Dose
Lisinopril (ACEI)10 mg once daily
Losartan (ARB)50 mg once daily
Amlodipine (CCB)5 mg once daily
Hydrochlorothiazide12.5–25 mg once daily

Note: Drug selection and titration must be individualized. Some drugs are contraindicated in pregnancy and pediatric patients.

Complications

  • Myocardial infarction
  • Stroke
  • Chronic kidney disease
  • Retinopathy
  • Heart failure (especially diastolic)

Follow-Up and Monitoring

  • Reassess BP every 1–3 months after initiation or adjustment of therapy.
  • Monitor for side effects (electrolyte changes, renal function, orthostatic hypotension).
