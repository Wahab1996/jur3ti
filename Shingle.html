Shingles (Herpes Zoster) Disease - 2025 Overview

What is Shingles?

  • Shingles is a viral disease caused by the reactivation of the varicella-zoster virus (the same virus that causes chickenpox).
  • It results in a painful, blistering skin rash, often on one side of the body or face.
  • Common in adults over 50 or those with weakened immune systems.

Symptoms & Signs

  • Painful or burning sensation, usually before the rash appears.
  • Red patches and fluid-filled blisters (usually confined to one dermatome/skin area).
  • Other symptoms: itching, numbness, headache, fever, and tiredness.

Complications & Risk Factors

  • Increased risk: age above 50, immunocompromised, stress, or recent illness.
  • Postherpetic neuralgia (chronic nerve pain).
  • Severe cases may involve the eyes (ocular shingles), ears (Ramsay Hunt syndrome), or spread internally in immunocompromised persons.

Is It Contagious?

  • Shingles itself is not directly contagious, but the virus can cause chickenpox in non-immune contacts through direct contact with blisters.
  • Not spread by coughing or sneezing.

Treatment

  • Early antiviral medicines (Acyclovir, Valacyclovir, Famciclovir), ideally within 72 hours of rash onset.
  • Pain management: NSAIDs, analgesics, topical lidocaine, or nerve pain medication (gabapentin, tricyclic antidepressants).
  • Specialist referral if eyes, face, or immunocompromised involvement.

Prevention

  • Highly effective shingles vaccines are recommended for adults aged 50+ to prevent shingles and its complications.

Latest Updates 2025

  • New protocols for post-exposure antiviral prophylaxis in high-risk contacts (e.g., newborns, pregnant women, immune compromised).
  • Expanded adult vaccination programs worldwide.
