Antihyperglycemic Treatment of Diabetes Mellitus

Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus (T1DM)

Insulin Replacement Therapy

  • Preferred methods: Multiple Daily Injections (MDI) or Insulin Pump.
  • Starting dose: 0.4–1.0 units/kg/day (usually 0.5), divided 50% basal + 50% prandial.
  • Adjust: Titrate based on glucose monitoring; reduce during “honeymoon phase.”
  • Educate: Teach carb counting and insulin-to-carb ratios.

Other Options

  • Pramlintide: Amylin analog (rarely used).
  • Pancreas or Islet Transplantation: For recurrent DKA or severe hypoglycemia despite optimal therapy.

Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (T2DM)

General Approach

  1. Start therapy at diagnosis with lifestyle + pharmacologic treatment.
  2. Metformin is first-line unless contraindicated.
  3. Reassess every 3–6 months; intensify if targets unmet.
  4. Consider insulin for HbA1c >10%, glucose ≥300 mg/dL, or catabolic symptoms.

Noninsulin Diabetes Medications

Drug Class Examples Key Points / Dosing
Biguanide Metformin Start 500 mg once or twice daily with food → titrate to 2000 mg/day. Avoid if eGFR < 30.
DPP-4 inhibitors Sitagliptin, Saxagliptin, Linagliptin Avoid saxagliptin in heart failure; do not combine with GLP-1RAs.
SGLT-2 inhibitors Empagliflozin, Dapagliflozin, Canagliflozin Cardiorenal benefits; continue if eGFR ≥20. Risk: genital infections, euglycemic DKA.
GLP-1 receptor agonists Semaglutide, Liraglutide, Dulaglutide Preferred in ASCVD, CKD, obesity; promotes weight loss. Start low, titrate slowly.
Sulfonylureas Glimepiride, Glipizide Start 1–2 mg/day, max 8 mg/day. Risk of hypoglycemia; avoid in elderly.
Thiazolidinedione (TZD) Pioglitazone 15–45 mg/day; contraindicated in CHF; beneficial in fatty liver disease.
Expected HbA1c reduction: ~1% for monotherapy; each added agent lowers HbA1c by 0.7–1.0%.

Insulin Regimens (Detailed)

1. Basal–Bolus Regimen

  • Composition: 50% basal + 50% bolus insulin (divided among meals).
  • Basal: Glargine, Detemir, Degludec.
  • Bolus: Lispro, Aspart, Glulisine.
  • Dosing: Start TDD 0.4–0.5 units/kg/day.
  • Example: 70 kg → 35 units/day (17 basal + 6 units before each meal).

2. Basal-Only Regimen

  • Indication: Fasting hyperglycemia, elderly, or new-onset T2DM.
  • Dose: 0.1–0.2 units/kg/day (e.g., Glargine at bedtime).
  • Titration: Increase by 2 units every 3 days until fasting glucose 80–130 mg/dL.

3. Premixed Regimen

  • Insulins: 70/30 NPH/Regular, 75/25 Lispro Mix, or 70/30 Aspart Mix.
  • Dosing: Twice daily — before breakfast (2/3) and dinner (1/3).
  • Starting: 0.3–0.5 units/kg/day.
  • Pros: Simpler dosing; Cons: Less flexible with meals.

4. Correctional (Sliding Scale) Regimen

  • Used: Hospitalized or acutely ill patients.
  • Example (Lispro):
    • 150–200 mg/dL → +2 units
    • 201–250 mg/dL → +4 units
    • 251–300 mg/dL → +6 units
    • >300 mg/dL → +8 units and recheck in 2 hrs
Note: Sliding-scale insulin should not be used alone. Always combine with basal insulin for stable glycemic control.

Choice of Antihyperglycemic Therapy in Comorbidities

Comorbidity Preferred Agents Avoid / Caution Rationale
ASCVD (Atherosclerotic Cardiovascular Disease) GLP-1 receptor agonists (Liraglutide, Semaglutide, Dulaglutide)
SGLT-2 inhibitors (Empagliflozin, Canagliflozin)		 Sulfonylureas, TZDs (may increase CV risk) Reduce major adverse cardiac events (MI, stroke, CV death)
Heart Failure (especially HFrEF) SGLT-2 inhibitors (Empagliflozin, Dapagliflozin) Thiazolidinediones (Pioglitazone) Decrease HF hospitalization and mortality
Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD) SGLT-2 inhibitors (Empagliflozin, Dapagliflozin)
GLP-1RA if eGFR < 30		 Metformin if eGFR < 30; Sulfonylureas in advanced CKD Slow kidney disease progression and reduce albuminuria
Obesity / Weight loss needed GLP-1RAs (Semaglutide, Liraglutide), SGLT-2 inhibitors Sulfonylureas, TZDs, Insulin Promote satiety and increase caloric loss in urine
Cost constraints Metformin, Sulfonylureas, TZDs Newer agents (GLP-1RA, SGLT2i) Affordable and accessible
Clinical tip: When multiple comorbidities exist, prioritize agents with overlapping benefits (e.g., SGLT2i for both CKD and HF).

Monitoring and Follow-Up

Glycemic Monitoring

  • HbA1c: Every 3 months if uncontrolled, every 6 months if stable.
  • Fasting glucose target: 80–130 mg/dL.
  • Postprandial (2-hr) glucose: < 180 mg/dL.
  • Continuous glucose monitoring (CGM): Recommended for intensive insulin users.

Blood Pressure and Lipids

  • BP goal: < 130/80 mmHg (ACE inhibitor or ARB preferred).
  • Lipid goal: LDL < 70 mg/dL (statin therapy for all adults ≥40 years).

Screening for Complications

  • Eyes: Annual dilated exam for retinopathy.
  • Kidneys: Urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio yearly.
  • Feet: Comprehensive foot exam yearly (more often if neuropathy).
  • Neuropathy: Assess vibration and monofilament sensation yearly.

Patient Education

  • Diet: Balanced carbohydrate intake, limited refined sugars.
  • Exercise: ≥150 min/week moderate-intensity.
  • Smoking cessation and weight management.
  • Vaccinations: Annual influenza + COVID-19; pneumococcal and hepatitis B as indicated.
Follow-up frequency: Typically every 3 months until stable, then every 6 months.
